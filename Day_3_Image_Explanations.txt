
This file explains the key images from Day 3 EDA.
Each image has a 3-line description and 2-line business guess.
Images are in the images/ folder.
Focus on 7 main visuals for clear insights.




1. top10_stores.png - Top 10 Stores by Total Sales
Explanation:
This bar chart shows the top 10 stores ranked by total sales over three years.
Store 262 leads with about 2 million euros in sales.
Other top stores range from 1 to 1.75 million euros each.
Business Guess:
These stores may be in busy city areas with more customers.
Invest more in them to keep high sales and learn from their success.



2. missing_values_heatmap.png - Missing Values Heatmap
Explanation:
This heatmap uses colors to show missing data in each column.
Blue means no missing values, like in Sales and Customers.
Yellow shows higher missing rates in Promo2 and Competition features, up to 80%.
Business Guess:
Many small stores skip Promo2, so focus on big ones for promotions.
Use flags for missing data to make models work better.



3. dow_sales.png - Average Sales by Day of Week
Explanation:
This bar chart plots average sales for each day from Monday to Sunday.
Monday and Saturday reach about 8,000 euros each.
Sunday is lowest at around 5,000 euros due to store closures.
Business Guess:
Plan more staff and ads for Monday and Saturday to boost peaks.
Test opening more stores on Sunday to raise low sales.



4. promo_effect.png - Average Sales: Promo vs No Promo
Explanation:
This bar chart compares sales on promo days to non-promo days.
Promo days average 8,228 euros, while non-promo days average 5,929 euros.
This shows a 39% increase from promotions.
Business Guess:
Run promotions more often to quickly lift sales across stores.
Pair them with busy days like Monday for even bigger gains.



5. monthly_sales.png - Average Daily Sales per Month
Explanation:
This bar chart shows average sales for each month from 2013 to 2015.
Most months stay around 6,000 euros.
December peaks at 7,500 euros due to holiday shopping.
Business Guess:
Stock more items in December to handle the sales rush.
Add summer promotions to lift flat months like July.



6. correlation.png - Correlation Matrix
Explanation:
This heatmap shows links between key features like Sales and Customers.
Sales and Customers have a strong positive link of 0.82.
Promo links to Sales at 0.37, while Day of Week shows a weak negative at -0.18.
Business Guess:
Drive more customers with ads, as they directly raise sales.
Use Promo in models but ignore weak links like Month.



7. storetype_sales.png - Sales Distribution by Store Type
Explanation:
This boxplot compares sales spread for store types a, b, c, and d.
Type b has the highest median at 7,000 euros and big outliers.
Type d has a tight range, showing steady but lower sales.
Business Guess:
Copy type b setups, like better stock, to other types.
Add promotions to type d to increase their steady sales.
